Financial markets

Anomaly detection for FICC markets

National space-agency research programme — 2021

4 anomaly methods built in-house
SDG 8 & 9 UN goal alignment

Technical lead on an explainable anomaly-detection programme for fixed income, currency, and commodity markets, delivered through a fintech analytics partner with national space-agency backing.

Approach. Four in-house detection methods combining correlation analysis and dynamic time warping with explanatory features (economic releases, rates, volatility), so anomalies were characterised rather than merely flagged. Delivered a real-time alerting prototype in the production platform.

Outcome. Secured competitive research funding; aligned to UN SDGs 8 and 9, targeting market integrity in higher-volatility developing markets.

Financial markets

Client defection / customer lifetime value

Fintech analytics platform — deployed to a tier-one investment bank sales desk (2018 onward)

Designed and built end-to-end — algorithm, UX, and layout — before the client’s engineers productionised it. Adapted from Bayesian BTYD methods in Python, ahead of equivalent open-source toolkits.

Approach. Probabilistic CLV modelling with a novel certainty metric and visualisation that turned attrition risk into something sales teams could act on directly.

Outcome. Real-time defection alerting in the platform’s front-office product; adopted by a tier-one investment bank sales team.

Financial markets

Seasonality and engagement forecasting

Fintech analytics platform — deployed to a tier-one investment bank sales desk (2021)

Built an STL-decomposition model forecasting daily-to-quarterly institutional client activity, end-to-end, with dashboard design before handover to the client’s engineers.

Approach. Seasonal-trend decomposition with probabilistic confidence bands, replacing a hard-coded rules-based approach and surfacing the next expected engagement window with calibrated uncertainty.

Outcome. Measurable accuracy gains over the incumbent tool; live on a tier-one investment bank sales desk from 2021.

Explainable NLP

Transformers for financial sentiment

Ongoing research direction

Applied research into financial sentiment classification with transformer models, focused on the accuracy–interpretability–efficiency trade-off for compliance-driven use.

Direction. Hybrid pipelines pairing contextual embeddings with dimensionality reduction (PCA, sparse PCA, UMAP) and interpretable classifiers (Random Forest, XGBoost), benchmarked against TF–IDF.
