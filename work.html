Financial market analytics

Anomaly detection for FICC markets

ESA & UK Space Agency research project — 2021

£30k ESA research funding
SDG 8 & 9 UN goal alignment

Technical lead on a research collaboration with ESA and UKSA on explainable anomaly detection for fixed income, currency, and commodity markets, delivered through a fintech analytics platform partner. The project drew on published novelty-detection research, with the team developing its own anomaly detection methods tailored to financial market and transaction data.

Approach. Four anomaly detection methods developed in-house, combining correlation analysis and dynamic time warping with explanatory features (economic releases, benchmark rates, volatility, market returns) so that each anomaly was characterised rather than merely flagged. Refactored existing detection models, designed the back-testing framework, delivered a real-time alerting prototype embedded in the production platform.

Outcome. £30,000 of ESA research funding secured for the engagement. Programme aligned to UN Sustainable Development Goals 8 and 9, with engagement in developing-market banking where higher volatility and lighter regulation make market-integrity analytics particularly valuable.

Client defection / customer lifetime value

Fintech analytics platform — deployed to a tier-one investment bank sales desk (2018 onward)

Designed and built end-to-end: algorithm, UX, and final layout (in collaboration with a UI designer), before the client’s engineering team productionised it for ongoing support. Originated in pre-incorporation prototype work (2014) adapted from the Bayesian BTYD methodology, written in Python before the lifetimes package and equivalent open-source toolkits became available.

Approach. Probabilistic CLV modelling with a novel certainty metric and visualisation that translated attrition risk into a form sales teams could act on directly.

Outcome. Real-time defection alerting integrated into the analytics platform’s front-office product; adopted by a tier-one investment bank sales team.

Seasonality and engagement forecasting

Fintech analytics platform — deployed to a tier-one investment bank sales desk (2021)

Designed and built end-to-end: STL-decomposition forecasting model predicting daily, weekly, monthly, and quarterly institutional client activity. Coded from scratch, layout and dashboard design developed in collaboration with a UI designer, before handover to the client’s engineering team for production support.

Approach. Seasonal-trend decomposition with probabilistic confidence bands replacing the previous hard-coded rules-based approach, surfacing the next expected client engagement window with calibrated uncertainty.

Outcome. Demonstrated accuracy improvement over the incumbent forecasting tool on internal benchmarks; integrated into the platform in 2021 for live use by a tier-one investment bank sales desk.

Explainable NLP

Transformers for financial sentiment

Ongoing research direction

Applied research into financial sentiment classification using transformer-based language models. Focus is on the trade-off between accuracy, interpretability, and efficiency for time-sensitive, compliance-driven applications: where pure black-box approaches fail to satisfy regulatory and operational requirements, but pure lexicon-based methods leave meaningful accuracy on the table.

Direction. Hybrid pipelines combining contextual embeddings with dimensionality reduction (PCA, sparse PCA, UMAP) and interpretable downstream classifiers (Random Forest, XGBoost), evaluated against TF–IDF baselines on standard financial sentiment corpora.
