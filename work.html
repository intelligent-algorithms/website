Seasonal forecasting for an institutional analytics platform

Problem. The platform tracked client engagement but could not anticipate it. Standard forecasting did not fit data driven by season and the rhythms of institutional activity, and the forecasts it produced were too coarse to act on — the desk had no reliable way to know when a client was next likely to engage.

Approach. We matched the method to the structure of the data, separating each client’s engagement into its recurring seasonal shape and the slower drift beneath it, and forecasting across daily and monthly horizons. Dashboards flagged when each client was next expected to engage, enabling timely, personalised outreach.

  • Time-series forecasting
  • Calibrated uncertainty
  • Client analytics

Explainable anomaly detection for financial markets

Problem. Markets generate vast, fast-moving streams of data in which the rare events that matter are easily lost in noise. Conventional monitoring could flag that something unusual had happened but not explain why, leaving analysts to investigate each alert from scratch — and to do so transparently enough to be trusted in a compliance-sensitive setting.

Approach. We modelled the market itself to establish a baseline, then correlated detected anomalies against client positioning, and engineered explanatory features so that each alert was characterised, not merely raised. We designed the back-testing framework and delivered a real-time alerting prototype embedded in the client’s platform. The capability was scoped for developing markets, where market-integrity analytics matter most — aligning the work with UN Sustainable Development Goals 8 and 9.

  • Anomaly detection
  • Explainable AI
  • Market surveillance

Client defection modelling for a front-office sales desk

Problem. Institutional sales desks hold rich records of client activity but struggle to turn them into a forward view of which relationships are at risk. Raw activity counts mislead: a client who has gone quiet may be perfectly healthy, while one who looks active may be drifting away. The desk needed a principled, trustworthy way to see which clients were most valuable and most likely to defect, early enough to act.

Approach. We built a principled, interpretable model of which clients were most valuable and most likely to lapse — needing only how recently and how often each had engaged — with calibrated confidence, probabilistic visualisation, and real-time alerts built into the desk’s front-office tools.

  • Customer lifetime value
  • Churn & defection
  • Real-time alerting
