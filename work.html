Financial market analytics

Anomaly detection for FICC markets

European Space Agency & UK Space Agency — 2020 to 2021

£90k ESA research funding
3 rounds Competitive awards
SDG 8 & 9 UN goal alignment

Technical lead on the Mosaic Smart Data research collaboration with ESA and UKSA on explainable anomaly detection for fixed income, currency, and commodity markets. The work extended ESA’s Dr MUST novelty detection methodology, originally developed for satellite telemetry, into financial market and transaction data.

Approach. Correlation and dynamic time warping fitted to market data, with explanatory features (economic releases, benchmark rates, volatility, market returns) layered to characterise rather than merely flag each anomaly. Refactored existing detection models, designed the back-testing framework, delivered a real-time alerting prototype embedded in the production platform.

Outcome. Three consecutive competitive funding rounds at £30,000 each. Programme aligned to UN Sustainable Development Goals 8 and 9, with engagement in developing-market banking where higher volatility and lighter regulation make market-integrity analytics particularly valuable.

Client defection / customer lifetime value

Mosaic Smart Data platform — deployed for J.P. Morgan sales (2018 onward)

Designed and built end-to-end: algorithm, UX, and final layout (in collaboration with a UI designer), before Mosaic’s engineering team productionised it for support. Originated in pre-incorporation prototype work (2014) adapted from the Bayesian BTYD methodology, written in Python before the lifetimes package and equivalent open-source toolkits became available.

Approach. Probabilistic CLV modelling with a novel certainty metric and visualisation that translated attrition risk into a form sales teams could act on directly.

Outcome. Real-time defection alerting integrated into Mosaic’s front-office platform; adopted by the J.P. Morgan sales team following Mosaic’s first major client engagement.

Seasonality and engagement forecasting

Mosaic Smart Data platform — deployed for J.P. Morgan sales (2021)

Designed and built end-to-end: STL-decomposition forecasting model predicting daily, weekly, monthly, and quarterly institutional client activity. Coded from scratch, layout and dashboard design developed in collaboration with a UI designer, before handover to Mosaic’s engineering team for production support.

Approach. Seasonal-trend decomposition with probabilistic confidence bands replacing the previous hard-coded rules-based approach, surfacing the next expected client engagement window with calibrated uncertainty.

Outcome. Demonstrated accuracy improvement over the incumbent forecasting tool on internal benchmarks; integrated into the Mosaic platform in 2021 for live use by the J.P. Morgan sales desk.

Explainable NLP

Transformers for financial sentiment

Ongoing research direction

Applied research into financial sentiment classification using transformer-based language models. Focus is on the trade-off between accuracy, interpretability, and efficiency for time-sensitive, compliance-driven applications: where pure black-box approaches fail to satisfy regulatory and operational requirements, but pure lexicon-based methods leave meaningful accuracy on the table.

Direction. Hybrid pipelines combining contextual embeddings with dimensionality reduction (PCA, sparse PCA, UMAP) and interpretable downstream classifiers (Random Forest, XGBoost), evaluated against TF–IDF baselines on standard financial sentiment corpora.

Combinatorial optimisation

Tabu thresholding for the Frequency Assignment Problem

PhD research, University of Wales College of Cardiff — commissioned for Combat Net Radio test scenarios

Co-developed surrogate constraint tabu thresholding for radio frequency assignment under realistic interference constraints. Introduced adaptive memory structures, dynamic constraint relaxation, and threshold-based exploration control.

Publications. Annals of Operations Research (1996, 1999); Meta-Heuristics: Theory and Applications (Springer, 2001). Full list on the publications page.
