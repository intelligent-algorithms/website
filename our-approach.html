How we work

We start with the problem, not the technique.

We start with the problem, not the technique. Every engagement begins by working closely with the client to understand what they are really trying to achieve, the process they want to improve, the decision they need to make, the question they cannot yet answer. The domain does not matter to us. The mathematics of machine learning, optimisation, and statistical reasoning transfers across fields, and we have applied it in finance, environmental and satellite data, telecommunications, defence, and beyond. What stays constant is the discipline of understanding the problem before reaching for a method.

From there, we look for the creative, often novel, route to a better answer. We are less interested in applying a standard tool than in finding the most effective, efficient, and insightful way to improve a process, drawing on statistical reasoning, attention to causality and external factors, and the domain knowledge that determines whether a result is genuinely meaningful or merely plausible. Good analysis is not only about the model. It is about asking what is really driving the data, what would change if circumstances changed, and what a result does and does not justify.

We build to assist, not to replace. The end product is a tool that helps people make better decisions: clear, interpretable, and well-visualised, so that the person using it understands not just what it recommends but why. We treat explainability and honest communication of results as part of the work, not an afterthought, because a result that cannot be understood or trusted will not be acted on, however sophisticated the method behind it.

We bring research rigour to production engineering. Our background spans both the academic discipline of peer-reviewed research and the practical demands of systems that have to run reliably for real clients. That combination, the insistence on evidence and honest evaluation, together with the pragmatism of building things that work, is what we offer. We test our assumptions, we compare against sensible baselines, and we are candid about what a method can and cannot do.

We work well alongside teams. Much of our most rewarding work has been collaborative: helping teams sharpen an approach, mentoring those keen to deepen their data science, and supervising students and early-career researchers, several of whom have gone on to leading universities and distinction-level results. We value environments where ideas are tested openly and where constructive, well-meant technical challenge is welcomed as the ordinary business of doing good work.

If you have a hard problem, in any domain, we would be glad to talk it through with you.
