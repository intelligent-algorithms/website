Selected dissertation supervision

Balancing Accuracy, Interpretability and Efficiency in Financial Sentiment Analysis

MMath, Durham — First Class

Benchmarked PCA, Sparse PCA, and UMAP as (i) feature selectors for TF–IDF models and (ii) projection layers for FinBERT embeddings on the Financial PhraseBank corpus. Introduced a hybrid pipeline applying UMAP to FinBERT embeddings followed by Random Forest / XGBoost classification, retaining contextual richness while improving efficiency and interpretability.

Result. FinBERT + Sparse PCA + XGBoost achieved Macro-F1 of 92.5%, outperforming all TF–IDF pipelines and clarifying the accuracy–interpretability–efficiency trade-off relevant to time-sensitive, compliance-driven financial applications.

Approach

Supervision is research-led rather than syllabus-led. Students are expected to formulate the problem, justify the method, and defend the result. Feedback emphasises reproducibility, clarity of argument, and willingness to challenge default choices in their chosen specification. Curriculum adapts across multiple A-level boards and university dissertation frameworks.
