Research

Doctoral and post-doctoral research.

Three peer-reviewed papers applying tabu search and tabu thresholding to the radio frequency assignment problem — an NP-hard problem in military and civil communications. Joint with N. M. Stephens and S. Hurley.

Building on Glover’s tabu search framework, the work developed parameter regimes that perform on realistic instances and extended tabu thresholding with surrogate constraint handling. Carried out from 1994; published in Annals of Operations Research and Springer’s Meta-Heuristics volume. Full citations are on the publications page.

A tabu search algorithm for frequency assignment

Castelino, Hurley & Stephens. Annals of Operations Research, 63(2):301–319, 1996.

Applied tabu search with Glover’s recency- and frequency-based memory to six realistic instances (up to 726 links and 75,306 constraints) derived from Combat Net Radio scenarios.

Result. Lower interference and variance than a parallel genetic algorithm and a steepest-descent heuristic across all six problem sizes, with the largest gains on the largest instances.

A surrogate constraint tabu thresholding implementation for the Frequency Assignment Problem

Castelino & Stephens. Annals of Operations Research, 86:259–270, 1999.

Replaced explicit recency/frequency memory with tabu thresholding and added surrogate constraint handling for problems with hard and soft constraints.

Result. Matched or improved on the 1996 results while removing the parameter-tuning burden.

Tabu thresholding for the Frequency Assignment Problem

Castelino & Stephens. In I. H. Osman & J. P. Kelly (Eds.), Meta-Heuristics: Theory and Applications. Boston, MA: Springer, 2001.

Book-chapter consolidation placing tabu thresholding in the wider metaheuristics context, and arguing that threshold-based memory has practical advantages where parameter tuning is itself costly.

With acknowledgement to Fred Glover, whose tabu search framework underpins this work; the papers extend and apply his concepts, developed in dialogue with him.
