Research

Doctoral and post-doctoral research.

Three peer-reviewed papers in adaptive metaheuristics and applied combinatorial optimisation, joint with N. M. Stephens and S. Hurley. The papers apply tabu search and tabu thresholding to the radio link frequency assignment problem, an NP-hard combinatorial problem arising in military and civil communications.

Glover originated tabu search, the adaptive memory concepts of recency and frequency, threshold control, and strategic oscillation. The contribution of these papers is in applying those concepts to the frequency assignment problem, developing parameter regimes that work on realistic instances, and (in the second paper) extending tabu thresholding with surrogate constraint handling. The work was carried out 1994 and published in Annals of Operations Research and in the Springer Meta-Heuristics volume.

The papers are now behind journal paywalls. The summaries below describe what each paper did. Full citations are on the publications page.

A tabu search algorithm for frequency assignment

Castelino, Hurley & Stephens. Annals of Operations Research, 63(2):301–319, 1996.

Application of tabu search to the radio link frequency assignment problem, using Glover’s framework of adaptive recency- and frequency-based memory. Test problems were six computer-generated realistic instances of up to 726 links and 75,306 constraints, derived from Combat Net Radio assignment scenarios provided by the Defence Research Agency at Malvern and the Army EMC Agency at Blandford.

Approach. Tabu moves were determined by recency and frequency thresholds (r and s parameters), with aspiration criteria permitting tabu moves that improved on the best solution found. The objective function counted constraint violations; two-dimensional indexing tables reduced full evaluation from O(CNF) to O(CF).

Results. Comparison against a parallel genetic algorithm and a local steepest descent heuristic, each given the same wall-clock budget per test problem. Tabu search produced lower best interference and lower standard deviations across all six test sizes, with the largest margins on the larger problems. Robust to small variation in the recency and frequency parameters.

A surrogate constraint tabu thresholding implementation for the Frequency Assignment Problem

Castelino & Stephens. Annals of Operations Research, 86:259–270, 1999.

Extended the earlier tabu search work by replacing recency- and frequency-based memory with tabu thresholding (Glover, 1995), which uses thresholds on the objective function to control the acceptance of moves rather than explicit memory structures, and integrated surrogate constraint handling for problems with hard and soft constraints. Tested on the same set of computer-generated frequency assignment instances.

Contribution. Surrogate constraints aggregated multiple hard constraints into composite constraints that the search could trade off against the soft objective. The threshold control mechanism replaced the recency and frequency parameter tuning required in the earlier work, making the method easier to apply across different problem instances without re-tuning.

Results. Tabu thresholding with surrogate constraints matched or improved on the tabu search results from the 1996 paper while removing the parameter-tuning burden, on the same six test problems.

Tabu thresholding for the Frequency Assignment Problem

Castelino & Stephens. In I. H. Osman & J. P. Kelly (Eds.), Meta-Heuristics: Theory and Applications. Boston, MA: Springer, 2001.

Book-chapter consolidation of the tabu thresholding work, placing the method in the broader context of metaheuristics for combinatorial optimisation. Discusses the relationship between tabu thresholding and other adaptive memory techniques, including Glover’s strategic oscillation framework, and describes the practical engineering of the implementation.

Position. The chapter argues that for problems where parameter tuning is itself expensive (large instances, limited evaluation budgets), threshold-based memory has practical advantages over recency- and frequency-based memory, while preserving the search behaviour that makes tabu methods effective.

Acknowledgement: this body of work would not have been possible without the framework developed by Fred Glover. Where the papers introduce new ideas, those ideas are extensions and applications of his concepts to a specific problem domain, in dialogue with him during the research.
