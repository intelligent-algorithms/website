About

The practice, and the person behind it.

Intelligent Algorithms Ltd is a research and engineering consultancy founded by Dr Diane Castelino. The practice is small by design: senior, hands-on, and selective about engagement.

The company

Incorporated in 2018, formalising an advisory relationship with Mosaic Smart Data that began in 2014. The pre-incorporation period covered prototype development, academic partnership building, and the foundational thought-leadership writing encouraging investment banks to apply predictive analytics to their own transaction data.

Engagements since incorporation have included production analytics delivery for the Mosaic platform, ESA / UK Space Agency research funding for explainable anomaly detection, and academic partnerships with Oxford, UCL, and Cambridge.

The founder

Dr Diane Castelino read Computing and Statistics at the University of Wales College of Cardiff, and completed a PhD in Computer Science in the High Performance Computing Group on adaptive metaheuristics for combinatorial optimisation. Doctoral work was published in Annals of Operations Research and in the Springer Meta-Heuristics volume.

Subsequent industry experience spans full-time engineering at Deutsche Bank Equities IT (straight-through processing, FIX, CORBA, Java, C++), consultancy at Lehman Brothers and Deutsche Bank Commodities IT through DJC IT Consultancy Ltd, and the present practice under Intelligent Algorithms Ltd.

Current research interests

  • Adaptive metaheuristics and constraint-handling in combinatorial optimisation.
  • Explainable ML for financial sentiment, novelty detection, and market surveillance.
  • Dimensionality reduction as a bridge between contextual embeddings and interpretable classifiers.
  • Bayesian deep learning for environmental forecasting (sea-ice, oceanographic remote sensing).
  • Industry–academia translation of quantitative methods.

Approach

Work is grounded in the principle that production-grade quantitative systems benefit from research discipline, and that research benefits from production constraints. Engagements typically involve direct hands-on coding, design collaboration with client teams, and clear handover documentation to internal engineering.

Teaching and supervision

Concurrent with consulting work, the practice has included sustained research supervision: A-level Computer Science curriculum, undergraduate project guidance, and MSc dissertation supervision across applied ML and financial data science. Active supervision is currently paused.
